# Gravitational Faraday Effect

## Overview

This repository explores the **Gravitational Faraday Effect** — the phenomenon of light polarization rotation in curved spacetimes. We investigate how the curvature of spacetime, described by Einstein's general relativity, affects the polarization state of electromagnetic waves as they propagate through gravitational fields.

The project includes rigorous mathematical derivations of Riemann tensor corrections to third order in the geodesic frame using the powerful **xAct** computer algebra system for Mathematica. This code was written during that project. 

## Project Description
This project was funded by *DAAD, Deutscher Akademischer Austauschdienst* under the DAAD-WISE program for a research internship at the Max Planck Institute for Gravitational Physics (Albert-Einstein Institute), Postdam-Golm, Germany under the supervision of *Dr. Sajad Aghapour* during the period May 05.2024 - July 31. 2024.

The Gravitational Faraday Effect is a relativistic analog of the classical Faraday effect, where a magnetic field rotates the plane of polarization of light. In the gravitational case, the spacetime curvature plays the role of the "field," inducing rotation of light polarization as photons travel through curved spacetimes.

- Calculate Riemann tensor corrections to third order in the geodesic expansion
- Understand the physical implications for astrophysics and cosmology
- Provide computational tools for exploring these effects

## Physics Background

### Faraday Effect in Flat Spacetime
In classical electromagnetism, the Faraday effect describes the rotation of polarization vectors when light propagates through a medium in a magnetic field.

### Gravitational Analog
In curved spacetime, the metric tensor and Riemann curvature tensor induce similar effects on electromagnetic wave polarization. This is particularly important for:

- **Gravitational Lensing**: Understanding light deflection in strong gravitational fields
- **Gravitational Wave Detection**: Analyzing the coupling between gravitational and electromagnetic waves
- **Spin-gravity Coupling**: Motivation to study other spin coupling to gravity
- **Gravitational Holonomy**: Exploring the links to the topological and geometrical phases due to coupling to curvature.
